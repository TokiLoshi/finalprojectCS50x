Go Flexitarian

Meat consumption can have a huge impact on the environment. For each 1kg of beef produced, roughly 100 kg of CO2 equivalent emissions is released. Beef is also resource intensive, not only in terms of land usage and feed but also water. It takes around 3,698 gallons of water (78 full bathtubs) to produce just shy of 1kg of beef. Do you accept the challenge of substituting a meat dish for a vegetarian option this week?

Change out your lightbulbs:

Switching from incandescent to LED lighting is great for the environment and your wallet. LEDs use 75% less energy and last 25 times longer than incandescent bulbs. Changing your incandescent bulbs will help lower your energy bill and reduce your carbon footprint. We challenge you to pick a room in your home with incandescent lights and swap them out for LED lighting.

Make a commute green:

Need to run an errand, or are you hoping to shake up your commute? Consider taking a bicycle or walking instead, and start wracking up some green miles. Making your commute Green is great for the environment and helps you to reduce your carbon footprint. Committing to more green miles will get you outdoors and moving. Who knows, a healthy dose of endorphins may be just the shake-up your commute needs.

Sustainable fashion:

It takes around 3,780 liters and 33.4kg of CO2 equivalent in emissions to produce a single pair of jeans. Less than 1% of used clothing gets recycled into new garments. We challenge you to find more creative ways to shop and recycle your garments.

Bring your water bottle with you:

Ditch single-use plastics for a day. You might be surprised by how much you might save (plastic and money).

Bring reusable bags with you:

It takes roughly 20 years for a plastic bag to degrade. Plastic bags only partially break down into microplastics, which can end up in our drinking water and food. We challenge you to take reusable bags the next time you go shopping.
